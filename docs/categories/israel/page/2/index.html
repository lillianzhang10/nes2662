Part Two Shimeah and Naarah arrived at the vineyard. Healthy grapevines trailed along stone-free ground, and a thorny hedge surrounded the area to protect them from being trampled. Further up the hill, there was a juice-stained winepress and a stone watchtower. Some clusters of grapes remained here and there, but most of them had been harvested and processed into wine. A husband and wife stood by the winepress, loading skins and jars of fermenting wine onto a donkey.

Blog Post #3

November 6, 2017 Lillian Zhang

5 minute read

Part One Shimeah awoke as the horizon was starting to brighten with dawn light. He sat up from his sleeping pallet and surveyed the room. This dimly-lit chamber was on the second floor of his three-room house, and it served as the sleeping quarters for him and his mother, Naarah. Aside from him, the room was empty. His mother probably woke earlier to grind flour and knead dough. The pleasant scent of baking bread wafted upwards from the first floor; drawn by it, he climbed down the wooden ladder and headed over to the food preparation area.
